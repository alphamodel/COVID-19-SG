22 NEW CASES OF LOCALLY TRANSMITTED COVID-19 INFECTION


Published Date: 23 May 2021

1.     As of 23 May 2021, 12pm, the Ministry of Health (MOH) has
preliminarily confirmed 22 new cases of locally transmitted COVID-19
infection. 19 are linked to previous cases, amongst which 12 have
already been placed on quarantine. The remaining 3 are currently
unlinked. Based on our investigations so far, 21 are in the community,
and 1 resides in a dormitory.

2.     In addition, there are 3 imported cases, who have already been
placed on Stay-Home Notice upon arrival in Singapore. Of these, 2 are
returning Singaporeans or Singapore Permanent Residents.

3.     In total, there are 25 new cases of COVID-19 infection in
Singapore today.

4.     We are still working through the details of the cases, and
further updates will be shared via the MOH press release that will be
issued tonight.

MINISTRY OF HEALTH

23 MAY 2021
